# 🚀 Launching / porting

Quick Comp is "case B" of the ALTO launch doctrine: **a distinct product with
its own repo** that copies the ALTO engine's PATTERNS. ALTO is always the
master; fixes are ported from there to here.

## Porting from ALTO (the standing checklist)

1. ☐ Review ALTO's git log since the last sync; port PATTERN by PATTERN
   (never whole files — the codebases have diverged on purpose).
2. ☐ One ported item per commit, smallest change that works, tested live in a
   real browser before committing.
3. ☐ Adapt copy to realtors in English (with the Spanish mirror) — the
   STRUCTURE is what must match ALTO, not the words.
4. ☐ Update this playbook in the same commit.

## Launching a new market (ads)

Quick Comp sells nationwide from day one — the "vertical" equivalent here is
the TARGET MARKET. Before spending on ads in a state, check the two comp-data
briefs (Market Intelligence Briefs 1 & 2): full-disclosure states demo
tightest; in Texas and other non-disclosure states the pitch is the range +
confidence + county tax record, never pinpoint accuracy.

Priority ad markets (accuracy × realtor population × bilingual edge):
Florida → Arizona → Nevada → Georgia/NC → Illinois → California. Texas sells
through the warm network with the honest-range pitch.

## Checklist — new-market ad push

1. ☐ Creatives (image + video) showing the app valuing a home IN THAT MARKET
   (use a disclosure-state address for the tight-range wow).
2. ☐ Meta campaign pointing at the landing (or WhatsApp — see 03-ghl).
3. ☐ GHL workflows already cover the leads (they land on alto-ventas); check
   the bot script mentions the product correctly (02-team, 03-ghl).
4. ☐ Closer trained on the deck (04-sales); everything else is unchanged.

## The operating goal

One launch = **4 VAs + ad budget**: closer, appointment setter, onboarding,
customer service. Everything they need already exists: closer portal (create
account + deck + payment links), guided onboarding, /cs with tasks, leads
panel with mini-CRM, GHL notifications. The owner only watches ad spend and
execution.
